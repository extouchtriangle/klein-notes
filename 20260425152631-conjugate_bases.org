#+title: Anionic Base Stability (ARIO)
[[id:1a10a19d-430a-456c-ba63-57845d697b04][Acids/Bases]]

* ARIO: Anionic base stability
Atom (A)
- More electronegative atoms form more stable anions
- In the same group, larger anions are more stable because they better stabilize the charge
Resonance (R)
- Delocalized/resonance-stabilized charges are more stable
Induction (I)
- Electron-withdrawing groups ($NO_2$, $X$) stablize anions
Orbitals (O)
- Orbitals with more $s$-character are more stable ($sp$ is more stable than $sp^2$)
