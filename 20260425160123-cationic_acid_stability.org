#+title: Cationic Acid Stability
[[id:1a10a19d-430a-456c-ba63-57845d697b04][Acids/Bases]]

* Cation stability
- Only the A in ARIO ([[id:96641723-8644-4a3c-bbd0-51312e2d63e0][Anionic Base Stability (ARIO)]]) matters here
- More electronegative atom with positive charge --> stronger acid
  - Example: $NH_4^+$ is less acidic than $H_3O^+$
- More stable conjugate base --> weaker acid
  - Example: methylammonium ion is less acidic than aryl ammonium ion (resonance stabilized lone pair)
